Background

During our freshman year at Georgia Tech as members of Grand Challenges Living Learning Community, we began searching for a problem that harshly affects society. After several weeks of exploring various problem spaces, we chose the prevention of pressure injuries because they are extremely painful, dangerous, and costly. We chose to focus on prevention rather than treatment because pressure injuries can progress quickly once developed, and current treatments cost about $150,000 on average. By confronting pressure injury development, we hope to eliminate many of these costs, prevent human suffering, and save lives.

EACH YEAR IN THE US, 2.5 MILLION PATIENTS DEVELOP PRESSURE INJURIES AND 60 THOUSAND DIE PEOPLE DIE FROM THEM

Pressure Injuries

Pressure injuries develop when someone has been in the same immobile position for an extended period of time. The constant pressure on their body leads to skin breakdown, tissue damage, and sometimes infection. These are very prevalent problems in hospitals, nursing homes, and patients in long-term at-home care as they are often immobile for long periods of time. People often come into a hospital with a separate injury or condition, and when forced to become immobile in order to treat it or heal, they can develop these pressure injuries.

Gaps In Current Solutions

Currently, healthcare workers follow a cycle in which they rotate patients every two hours in order to alleviate and change where the pressure is on the body. However, this cycle is based on old traditions and not founded in science, so it frequently not enough to prevent the development of pressure injuries. In order to find out exactly what time patients need to be rotated, it is possible to use pressure mats which lie under the patient and detect the areas of higher pressure. However, these pressure mats are extremely expensive and not feasible for large-scale use. The 2-hour rotation cycle is also becoming less feasible as the increasing number of general patients in healthcare facilities is increasing, leaving more patients per healthcare worker.

The Increasing Relevance of Pressure injuries

The size of older age groups is currently increasing, which are the groups most susceptible to pressure injuries. Due to the COVID-19 Pandemic, the number of healthcare workers has been decreasing and the number of patients has spiked.

OUR SOLUTION: Pressure Tracking App

Our current solution uses a neural network to create a digital pressure map of a person using only a depth camera. Once this pressure map is created, our algorithm will calculate the risk of pressure injury based on time and pressure. In the future, we hope to include other factors that contribute to pressure injuries like moisture, heat, nutrition, and age. When the calculated pressure injury risk surpasses a predefined threshold, our app will send an alert to nurses or nursing home workers telling them to move the patient off of the at-risk body part. Our app will contain the patient's health information, graphs of pressure data, and a colored pressure map so that nurses can see areas of risk in greater detail. This will allow healthcare workers to be much more efficient in their rotation of patients, and allow the rotations to be patient-specific so that the risk of developing a pressure injury is significantly decreased.
